Politically Incorrect with Bill Maher

After working on various television shows and series, Bill Maher began hosting a late-night political talk show ‘Politically Incorrect’. He started hosting on 25th July 1993 which was originally broadcasted on Comedy Central but later shifted to ABC Network. It was an American political talk show. This show began with a topical monologue by Bill preceding the introduction of guests. Likewise, this show invites four guests with diverse professions like show business, popular culture, political pundits, political consultants, authors, and news figures. They would discuss the issue selected by Bill.

Real-Time with Bill Maher

After leaving his previous show, Bill started his talk show ‘Real Time with Bill Maher’ in the year 2003. He became the host, co-producer, and co-writer of the weekly political comedy talk show airing on the cable television network HBO. Unlike the previous show, this show is a serious and well-informed guest show program. This program is a one-on-one interview with guests in-studio or via satellite. Likewise, in this show, Bill mainly sits with two or three panelists consisting of authors, activists, actors, politicians, pundits, and journalists discussing weekly events.

Controversies

Throughout his successful career as a host and a political commentator, Bill has come across many controversial matters. During his show ‘Politically Incorrect,’ he made a controversial on-air remark on the September 11 attack. His agreed statement on the 9/11 attack made by conservative pundit guest Dinesh D’Souza rose to controversy. He defended President Bush’s statement of calling the terrorist coward saying, ‘We have been the cowards. Lobbing cruise missiles from 2,000 miles away. That’s cowardly. Staying in the airplane when it hits the building. Say what you want about it. Not cowardly. You’re right.’ However, he later clarified the statement about it not being anti-military in any way referencing his well-documented longstanding support for the American military.

However, this was not his only controversy. In the same year, he apologized for comparing his dogs to retarded children. Moreover, his controversial matter did not end there. Bill made a notable remark after the military missed its recruiting goals by 42 percent. The Alabama Congressman Spencer Bachus felt the comments were demeaning to the military and treasonous. However, defending this controversy, Bill stated his support and highest regard for the military. Furthermore, he also asked why the congressman criticized him instead of focusing on the recruitment problem.

Likewise, in June 2017, Bill came under criticism after people called on HBO to fire him. He said ‘I’m a house nigger’ on Real-Time. Personally, Bill conflicted with his then-girlfriend after she sued him for $9 million for inflicting pain and suffering on her. However, the lawsuit was later withdrawn in the year 2005.
