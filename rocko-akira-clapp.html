Introducing the Knoxville-Nelson Power Duo

Rocko Akira Clapp’s star-studded lineage stems from his father, the multi-talented Johnny Knoxville, and his mother, Naomi Nelson. Johnny Knoxville, known by his stage name but born Philip John Clapp, has dazzled audiences for nearly a decade with his acting prowess, comedic genius, and daring stunts.

Knoxville soared to fame as a co-creator and star of the MTV reality stunt show “Jackass,” which captivated viewers for three thrilling seasons from 2000 to 2001. Beyond television, Knoxville’s cinematic journey includes blockbuster hits like “Men in Black II,” “The Dukes of Hazzard,” and “The Last Stand.”

Naomi Nelson, Rocko’s mother, might have entered the limelight through her marriage to Johnny Knoxville, but she’s made her mark as an assistant, director, and producer in the entertainment industry. Although Naomi prefers to work behind the scenes, her influence is undeniable, with projects like “Fifth Star” and “Carnivàle” to her credit.
