Who Are the Band Members of the Runaways?

The Runaways were an all-female rock band from Los Angeles formed in 1975. They made waves in the music industry with their rebellious attitude and energetic performances. Over the years, the band went through several lineup changes, but their impact and influence remain undeniable.

The original members of the Runaways included Joan Jett, Sandy West, Micki Steele, Lita Ford, and Cherie Currie. These talented musicians brought their unique style and personality to the band, creating a powerful sound that captivated audiences.

Throughout their career, the Runaways experienced changes in their lineup, with members joining and replacing each other. Notable additions and replacements included Peggy Foster, Jackie Fox, Vicki Blue, and Laurie McAllister.

The Runaways’ iconic lineup of Joan Jett, Sandy West, Cherie Currie, Lita Ford, and Jackie Fox became synonymous with the band’s success. Together, they released several albums and gained popularity not only in the United States, but also overseas, particularly in Japan.

Formation of the Runaways

The Runaways, an iconic punk rock band, were formed in August 1975 by Rock Impresario Kim Fowley. Fowley had an innovative vision for an all-female rock band at a time when there were very few young girls playing instruments in the music industry.

The band’s initial lineup consisted of Joan Jett and Sandy West, who were joined shortly after by Micki Steele, Lita Ford, and Cherie Currie. With Fowley’s assistance in the recruitment process, the members of the Runaways came together, ready to make their mark on the Los Angeles music scene.

The Runaways’ formation marked the beginning of an exciting chapter in rock history. Their fusion of punk attitude and rock ‘n’ roll sounds captured the attention of audiences and paved the way for future female rock bands.

The Rise to Fame

The Runaways quickly rose to fame with their electrifying performances and rebellious image. However, it was their breakthrough hit, “Cherry Bomb,” that propelled them to stardom and solidified their place in rock history. This song became synonymous with the band and showcased their raw energy and provocative lyrics.

The most iconic and well-known lineup of the Runaways consisted of lead vocalist and guitarist Joan Jett, drummer Sandy West, lead vocalist Cherie Currie, guitarist Lita Ford, and bassist Jackie Fox. Together, these talented musicians created a dynamic sound that captivated audiences worldwide.

The Runaways released several albums including their self-titled debut album “The Runaways,” “Queens of Noise,” and “Waitin’ for the Night.” These albums showcased the band’s unique blend of punk, hard rock, and glam influences, earning them a dedicated fanbase.

“We had the hard edge of punk rock, but we also had a pop sensibility. We wanted to shake things up and challenge the status quo.” – Joan Jett

With their rebellious spirit and daring lyrics, the Runaways made a lasting impact on the music scene of the late 1970s. Their provocative image and infectious energy attracted fans from all walks of life, and they became a sensation in Japan, where they achieved incredible success and fan adoration.

Post-Runaways Careers

After the breakup of the Runaways, the band members embarked on diverse paths in their music careers. Let’s take a look at their individual journeys:

Joan Jett

Joan Jett, one of the founding members of the Runaways, transitioned into a successful solo career. She soared to new heights with hits like “I Love Rock ‘n Roll,” becoming a rock icon in her own right. Joan Jett’s fierce attitude and rebellious spirit continued to captivate audiences around the world.

Lita Ford

Lita Ford, the band’s talented guitarist, also carved out a successful solo career after the Runaways. In the 1980s, she achieved commercial success with songs like “Kiss Me Deadly” and “Close My Eyes Forever,” a duet with Ozzy Osbourne. Lita Ford’s fiery guitar skills and powerful vocals established her as a force to be reckoned with in the rock music scene.

Cherie Currie

Cherie Currie, the lead vocalist of the Runaways, briefly pursued an acting career following the band’s breakup. She appeared in movies such as “Foxes” and “Wavelength” before refocusing on her music. Cherie Currie released several solo albums, showcasing her unique blend of rock and punk influences.

Sandy West

Sandy West, the band’s dynamic drummer, continued to pursue her passion for music. She collaborated with her former bandmates on various projects and remained active in the rock scene. Sandy West’s undeniable talent and infectious energy left a lasting impact on the Runaways’ sound.

Potential Reunion

There have been discussions and rumors of a potential reunion of the iconic girl band, the Runaways. While fans have eagerly awaited a reunion, it has yet to materialize in its entirety.

In 2010, a glimmer of hope arose when Joan Jett and Cherie Currie reunited to re-record their hit song “Cherry Bomb” for the popular video game Guitar Hero: Warriors of Rock. This collaboration reignited the excitement and nostalgia surrounding the band.

However, despite the enthusiasm from fans, a full reunion has not come to fruition. Lita Ford, another key member of the Runaways, has expressed reservations about joining a reunion and has cited timing issues as a factor.

While it may be disappointing to some, it seems unlikely that a complete Runaways reunion will happen in the future. Nevertheless, their music and influence continue to inspire generations of fans and aspiring musicians alike.

Legacy of the Runaways

The Runaways left a lasting legacy in the world of rock music, particularly due to their unique position as an all-female band in a predominantly male-dominated industry. Their fierce attitude and electrifying performances not only captivated audiences worldwide but also paved the way for future female-oriented punk bands.

The Runaways’ music continues to be celebrated and remains influential to this day. One of their most iconic and memorable songs, “Cherry Bomb,” still resonates with fans and showcases the band’s signature style. Its rebellious lyrics and energetic sound have solidified its place as a standout hit in the Runaways’ repertoire.

Individually, the band members have made significant contributions to the music industry, further solidifying their impact. Joan Jett, in particular, has had a successful solo career following the breakup of the Runaways. Her fearless attitude and rock ‘n’ roll spirit have made her an icon in her own right, and her influence can still be seen and felt in the industry today.

The Runaways’ legacy extends beyond their own music. Their groundbreaking status as an all-female band challenged norms and shattered barriers, inspiring countless aspiring musicians. Their fierce determination and unapologetically raw performances continue to serve as an inspiration for future generations of rock musicians, especially those who identify as women.
