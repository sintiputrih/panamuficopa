TJ Hockenson is a professional football player in the National Football League. Following his draft in 2019, he currently plays for the NFL team Detroit Lions. The footballer serves as a tight end for the Lions.

Besides that, he played collegiate football at the University of Iowa with the Iowa Hawkeyes. The athlete is an exceptional football player and has received several honors and awards for his skills.

In his junior year, T.J. won the John Mackey Award. It is awarded to the most outstanding tight end in college football. Touchdown Club of Columbus also presented him with Ozzie Newsome Award for his contribution to college football.

Moreover, the former Hawkeye was selected to play in the Pro Bowl in his second NFL year. Hockenson has already shown the Detroit team his true potential. He gets better with every game.

Although he suffered a serious injury in his first season with the Lions, he returned bigger and better. In the 2020 season, he caught a touchdown leading to Detroit’s third win.

TJ Hockenson | Football Career

High School Career

The athlete started his high school football journey with the Chariton Chargers. Along with football, he played high school basketball. Furthermore, he was a four-year varsity letter winner. 

The footballer served the Chargers as a tight end and defensive back. He assisted the school team in record-breaking wins. Moreover, he helped them reach the state finals and was the All-State First Team.

Popular college football websites considered Hock a Three-Star recruit and the Seventh-Highest Rated recruit. After an exceptional career with Chariton, he joined the University of Iowa to play collegiate football.

College Career

The former Chargers is very fond of Tampa Bay Buccaneers tight end Rob Gronkowski. His college tight-end coach Brian Frentz had a chance to coach the former Patriot player. So, he showed Thomas several clips on how Gronk explored his true potential.

Furthermore, Hawkeye was very impressed and inspired by Rob. As a redshirt, the tight end didn’t play his freshman year. 

Nevertheless, he proved his value to the Hawkeyes in his sophomore by recording several receptions and touchdowns. Besides that, the NFL player won several awards in his junior year. The footballer received the John Mackey Award and Ozzie Newsome Award in 2018.

Correspondingly, voters selected the Lions player to the First-team All-Big Ten that same year. T.J. also received Kwalick–Clark Tight End of the Year for his outstanding tight-end abilities. Furthermore, he didn’t play his senior year. Instead, he chose to enter the 2019 NFL Draft.

He broke the news through social media. After that, Hockenson thanked his college coach and Hawkeyes for allowing him to play with them. The athlete elaborated that NFL was his lifelong dream and felt it was time to pursue it. 

NFL Career

After entering the 2019 NFL Draft, the Detroit Lions drafted Thomas in the first round. Moreover, he was the eighth pick overall. The Lions signed a $19.8 million contract with the tight end for four years. 

He made his debut as a Lion in a game against the Arizona Cardinals. Furthermore, the former Hawkeye was the only tight end in NFL history to make 131 yards in their first game.

However, his first NFL season ended in horror when he endured a severe injury in his leg. Nevertheless, he has recovered and is back on the field. He was placed on the COVID-19 reserved list at the beginning of 2020.

Hock was allowed to play ten days later. Although 2020 wasn’t fruitful for many, he had a productive year career-wise. Along with having an outstanding season, the tight end made it to his first Pro Bowl.

Severe Leg and Ankle Injury

In Hockenson’s rookie season, he sustained a critical injury to his leg. The injury landed him in the hospital and eventually on the injured list. On the 2019 Thanksgiving game, Chicago Bears‘ linebacker Roquan Smith tackled him. 

During the altercation, T.J. got his leg stuck under his weight. As a result, he broke his leg and tore some ligaments in his ankle. He has recovered well and is actively playing for the Lions.
