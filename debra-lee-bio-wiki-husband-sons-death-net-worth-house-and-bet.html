Debra Lee  Career

Legal and early BET career

Lee has worked as a law clerk from 1981 to September 1981 to Barrington Parker of the U.S. She then joined BET as Vice President of BET’s legal affairs department and also general counsel in 1986 after more than five years as an attorney with Washington, D.C. based Steptoe & Johnson, a corporate law firm.

Lee has also served as BET’s corporate secretary and president and publisher of BET’s publishing division, which publishes Emerge BET Weekend including Heart & Soul magazines.

As CEO of BET Holdings

In March, the year 1996, Lee became the President and also Chief Operating Officer (COO) of BET Holdings, Inc., replacing departing network founder, named Robert L. Johnson. In 2005, Lee became President and Chief Executive Officer (CEO). The company had almost $100 million in revenue the other year.

Directorships

Lee serves on board of directors of Washington Gas Light Company as well as Marriott and Revion. He is also a member of the board of directors at the Monsanto subsidiary Genuity. Lee was added as a member of the board of directors of Twitter following her trial by returning CEO Jack Dorsey.

As Chief Executive Officer of Black Entertainment Television

Lee was the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of BET, Black Entertainment Television from 2005 to MAy 28, 2018. Debra increased the production budget of the company by 50%. She redesigned the company’s mission statement by naturing talents, supporting families, and encouraging their dreams.

Debra Lee House

After sitting on the market for nearly three years and facing price drops approximating $4 million, the Debra Lee was sold in 2019.Originally listed for $13.5 million, the luxurious single-family estate decreased in price several times over the years before finding a buyer earlier this month. Despite the cuts, the Massachusetts Heights home is the most expensive to go under contract in Washington this year, Urban Turf reports.

Marshall Moya Design, one of the architecture firms behind Audi Field (the newest home of the D.C. United soccer team), designed the expansive Rock Creek Park home, which spans approximately 11,000 square feet. The estate, listed by Washington Fine Properties, boasts amenities such as a movie theater, saltwater pool and a nine-car garage.

Why ditch her posh digs, which were last listed at $9.5 million? Lee initially put her mansion up for sale in 2016, a few months before BET announced its plans to move its headquarters to New York. When she left the area in 2017, Lee said she would be splitting her time between New York and Los Angeles, where her children live.
