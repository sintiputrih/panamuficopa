BIOGRAPHY OF PILOU ASBÆK

Pilou Asbæk is a Danish actor born March 2, 1982 in Copenhagen, Denmark as Johan Philip “Pilou” Asbæk. He is best known for his role as spin trouble doctor Kasper Juul in the Danish television political drama Borgen, and as Euron Greyjoy in the television series Game of Thrones

He went to Herlufsholm School where he was active in drama productions. He graduated from the National Theater School of Denmark in the summer of 2008. He is the son of gallery owners Maria Patricia (née Tonn) and Jacob A. He has two older brothers, Thomas Asbæk, an art consultant at Asbæk Art Consulting , and Martin Asbæk , gallerist at the Martin Asbæk Gallery.

In 2009, Asbæk played soldier David Grüner in a second season episode of another Danish TV series, The Killing. He had his breakthrough in 2010 as inmate Rune in Lindholm & Noer’s prison drama for which he won Best Actor at the Danish Critic Association Award, Bodil, and Danish Film Academy Awards, Robert.

In 2011, Asbæk starred in the film, R, as a Danish convict, depicting a harrowing prison experience. In 2012, he starred in another Tobias Lindholm film called A Hijacking, about Somali piracy, for which he transformed physically, gaining weight to imbue the role with a method game. In 2013, Asbæk portrayed a flamboyant Danish tycoon named Simon Spies in the movie Sex, Drugs & Taxation.

In 2014 he starred in Luc Besson’s film, Lucy and the same year he appeared in the film, Silent Heart, in which his mother-in-law, Danish actress Vigga Bro, plays the character of Lisbeth. In 2015, Asbæk collaborated with director Tobias Lindholm in the film, A War, playing a soldier in Afghanistan. In 2017, he played Batou in Ghost in the Shell.

Asbæk has also appeared in television series. He played Private David Grüner in an episode of the second season of another Danish TV series, The Killing. From 2010 to 2013, he played spin-doctor, Kasper Juul, in the critically acclaimed Danish TV series Borgen, which deals with the politics of a female Prime Minister of Denmark. In 2016, he joined the HBO series Game of Thrones in season 6 as the ruthless pirate captain Euron Greyjoy.
