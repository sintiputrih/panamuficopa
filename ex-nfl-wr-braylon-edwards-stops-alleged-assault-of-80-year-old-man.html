Ex-NFL WR Braylon Edwards Stops Alleged Assault of 80-Year-Old Man

Former NFL player Braylon Edwards recently made headlines after intervening in a distressing incident at a suburban Detroit YMCA. Here's a comprehensive report on the event:

The Incident

  • On Friday, Braylon Edwards, who played eight seasons in the NFL with teams like the Cleveland Browns and New York Jets, heard a dispute at the recreation center in Farmington Hills, Michigan, regarding loud music.
  • Upon hearing a thud, he turned around and witnessed a 25-year-old man allegedly assaulting an 80-year-old man in the locker room.
  • Edwards immediately intervened, putting a stop to the alleged assault and potentially saving the victim's life.

Legal Proceedings

  • The 25-year-old suspect fled the scene but was later captured by the police. He has been charged with attempted murder and remains in custody on a $250,000 bond.

Braylon Edwards' Response

  • Edwards, a star receiver at Michigan, downplayed his heroic actions, stating, "At the end of the day, that's what you do".
  • He recounted the incident, expressing concern for the victim and the unexpected turn of events that led him to intervene.
  • The names of the victim and the alleged assailant have not been made public. Police reported that the victim is still in the hospital but is expected to recover from his injuries.
