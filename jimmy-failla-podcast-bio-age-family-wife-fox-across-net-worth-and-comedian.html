Jimmy Failla Biography

Jimmy Failla is an American Stand-Up Comedian, TV Pundit, Author and Host who was born and brought-up in Levittown, New York. Currently, he works at FOX News Media, Fox News Radio, Fox Nation in New York City, New York as the HOST: Fox Across America. He is also a former New York City cab driver, he was named Outstanding Male Comedian of the year at the 2014 New York City Nightlife Awards.

Jimmy also works at A-List as the Head Writer, at BIG TOP PRESS as an Author and appeared at Gotham Comedy Club as a Stand-Up Comic. He previously worked as a Head Writer for Kennedy Show – FOX Business, Columnist for TheFederalist.Com and as a Radio Host for Off The Meter.

Jimmy Failla Fox Across America

Failla appears regularly on the Kennedy Show on Fox Business as well as also makes appearances on Redeye on Fox News and Strange Inheritance Unpacked. In addition, Failla has appeared on ESPN’s Dream Job (Season 1, Episode 1), The Today Show (October 8, 2008), BBC America’s Richard Hammond’s Crash Course (October 29, 2012), AXS Television’s Gotham Comedy Live (November 15, 2012), as well as Season 8 of NBC’s America’s Got Talent (July 2013).

His AGT appearance ended in controversy after Failla asked to be cut from the program in an open letter to producers, a development that became the lead story on Page 6 of the NY Post (July 13, 2013). The fallout had to do with a un-aired portion of his first-round audition in which Judge Mel B deemed one of Failla’s jokes “racist” only to have the crowd stage a revolt on his behalf.

Despite Mel’s protests, judges Howard Stern, Heidi Klum, along with Howie Mandel voted Failla on to the Las Vegas round, setting off a wild celebration at the Hammerstein Ballroom.

Although that set never aired, it was covered by over a hundred news outlets, including TMZ, Perez Hilton, as well as CBS’s The Talk. The incident also resurfaced on the Howard Stern Show later that summer when comic Lisa Lampanelli criticized Mel B as well as voiced her support of Failla.

Jimmy Failla Fox News Radio | Podcast

In June 2006 Failla made the first of countless appearances on Sirius XM’s Maxim Radio. He’s also appeared on The Covino as well as Rich Show and The Lisa Lampanelli Show on Howard 101.

In September 2010 Failla began co-hosting a critically acclaimed comedy podcast called The Specialists with international comedy giant Lee Camp as well as three-time Bunkenheimer Award Winner Kenneth Wood. The show racked over 100,000 listeners that fall after gaining a reputation for irreverent 30-second sketches as well as A-List guests, including Paul Provenza (The Aristocrats) Mo Rocca (CBS) along with John Fugelsang (CNN).

Jimmy Failla Comedian

His first professional shows took place at Times Square’s Ha! Comedy Club as well as Caroline’s on Broadway. Failla has since gone on to become a regular at all of the major clubs in the city including his home club, Gotham Comedy Club, and Comic Strip Live. He has shared the stage with countless big-name comics, including Jerry Seinfeld, Chris Rock, Jim Gaffigan, long with Dave Chappelle.
